Our Class

This project is a part of our INFO 465 - Time, Technology, and Design - class for the winter quarter of 2023. This course explores time as an element of human life, and how design and technology influences how people experience it. Some topics explored include machine time, crip time, temporal tensions, Afrofuturism, and music in time. Throughout the course of the quarter, we have each worked to fill an 80+ page sketchnook with sketches, notes/reflection, and collages. Our final group project allowed us to explore time, technology, and design in a creative and free way.

Our Project

Our project, Timeless Conversations, is an exploration of people's experiences of time. We interviewed and recorded 16 conversations with people of different ages and different cultural backgrounds. Using these conversations, we built a podcast exploring our interview questions and how our interviewees answered them.

In our project, time is the centerpiece of all our questions to our interviewees. We sought to see how time is perceived differently between individuals. This has allowed us to begin understanding others at a deeper level temporally. By gaining this understanding, we hope to further reach a level of empathy for how to best work with all different experiences of time.

Our Questions:

Below are our questions that we used to guide our interviews. Through this we had conversations surrounding each interviewee's perception of time. These questions were purposely broad to allow each person to interpret the question as they wish. Depending on our interviewees, we had to frame questions differently due to language barriers or due to young age.

  • How do you keep track of time?
    • Ex: Planners, to-do list
  • How do you feel when you miss a deadline or expectation (ex. Be somewhere on time)
    • Ex: late or missed a deadline
  • How do you feel when you have to wait for somebody?
  • How do you visualize time?
    • Time as a numerical or phase-type concept
  • Where are you in time right now?
    • Ex: "I'm 21", "It's 10:00", stage of life
