INFO 465 - Timeless Conversation

Our Project Our Story Our Reflections

Our Story

The Idea

Time is the core of life. Through this class, our group has further understood how time is experienced through how we move about our days, and is influenced by several factors. The Temporal Tensions activity revealed how different groups perceived tensions in their lives and prioritized those to design products that reduce such tensions. Our group was interested in how different people perceive, experience, and interact with time differently. Given the opportunity to freely and creatively explore time, technology, and design, our group decided to attempt to understand other people's perceptions of time. Through this, we wanted to understand other people at a deeper level to better empathize with and respect their time.

Our Initial Plan

Through discussion, we settled on a path for our project, A Timeless Conversation. We sought to explore different people's experiences of time through conversations with a diverse range of people. In hopes of creating visual content of some sort, we decided to have our interviewees draw on a piece of paper during the conversation.

With Gisele's previous experience of creating podcasts for WINFO, we set our goal deliverable to be a podcast incorporating portions of our interviews with our discussions and reflections. Having read about the importance of slow design and reflection, we found this as a great opportunity to not only interview and learn about other people's perceptions of time but also come together as a group to reflect and learn from each other.

Creating a podcast would allow our group to explore concepts learned from this class in a different medium than other classes. We all discussed how other classes often asked for papers, presentations, or prototypes to showcase our learnings from the course. As a group, we agreed on wanting to create and work on a unique medium to showcase our knowledge.

At this point in the quarter, we were still trying to gauge the scale of our project. How many interviewees would be appropriate? How could we balance the workload with other commitments? After discussing each other's schedules with each other, we settled on committing to complete an average of four interviews. Considering our goal was to hear from a diverse group of people, we each worked to identify four unique individuals from different cultural backgrounds, age groups, and genders.

Our Questions

Approaching our conversations from a semi-structured to fully-structured interview, our group ideated five main questions that would guide us in the interview process. These questions were kept broad to allow people to interpret and answer freely to better gain a sense of the person's interaction and experience with time. We also chose to give each interviewee a blank sheet of paper so that they could write down and sketch their thoughts over the course of the interview.

  • How do you keep track of time?
  • How do you feel when you miss a deadline or an expectation?
  • How do you feel when you have to wait for somebody?
  • How do you visualize time?
  • Where are you in time right now?

The Timeline

After having our idea, we turned to create a coherent schedule to allow plenty of time to interview our participants, review the interviews, create our reflective talk, and combine it all together into a podcast. This was important since our project relied heavily on scheduling time with other people to interview them. With only four weeks left in the quarter, it was crucial for us to begin reaching out to people.

The Week-by-Week Schedule

  • Week 6 - Reach out to interviewees
  • Week 7 - Conduct interviews
  • Week 8 - Outline narrative of conversation
  • Week 9 - Record our conversation and edit the podcast for an MVP
  • Week 10 - Finish the project and finalize details

Having ideated four individuals to interview, we shared the cultural, age, and gender backgrounds of our interviewees with each other to ensure that we covered a diverse range of people. While we were unable to capture every possible group, considering the time we had left and our schedules, we agreed that we had gathered a decent diversity of people.

Further Refining Our Goals

We discussed what would be necessary to create a high-quality podcast: interview audios that were clear and captured the interviewee's thoughts. To help us identify clips from the audio that capture key quotes from our interviews, we also discussed creating transcripts of our interviews. These interviews (with their respective audio and visual elements) served as our main focus for the following weeks.

The Interviews

After reaching out to participants, our group worked on conducting interviews using the questions that we ideated in previous weeks. During these interviews, we asked our participants to envision themselves in time and draw out how time relates to them, they answered our questions both verbally and on a piece of paper. Throughout the interview period, our group kept each other updated on the great insight and thoughts we were hearing from our interviewees that we could incorporate into our final conversation.

Our Interviewees

We kept track of our interviewees (including their demographics) in a Google Spreadsheet to keep each other updated on the progress of our interviews. This allowed us to stay communicative with each other to better understand each other's progress.

To the Conversation

The interview process took longer than expected, as scheduling with other people to conduct these conversations served difficult during the quarter. Furthermore, with a smaller set of questions, we realized that conversations ranged from five minutes to 15 minutes. Through this, the need for a reflection by us to serve as the main audio part that would guide the podcast became clear. Thus we began scheduling a time for the group the meet and converse on the insights and thoughts received from our interviewees to create a podcast narrating different people's perceptions of time based on our questions.

The Conversation

We met as a group to reflect on our interviews to frame the base of our podcast. During this time, we also discussed the next steps in how we wanted to approach our final product.

Narrative Framing

After all of the interviews were been conducted, the four of us got together and recorded a conversation, referencing the themes we noticed throughout our interviews, and what people talked/drew about. Our aim was to have this be the base of the podcast and add clips from the interviews as supplemental information.

Next Steps

After recording this conversation, our next steps were to:

  1. Stitch together our interviews and insights into a cohesive podcast
    • We began looking into AI podcast editing software that would help create a smooth flow of conversation for the podcast by taking out awkward pauses, overlapping of speakers, and filler words.
    • We began looking at key insights and areas in the conversation where audio from interviews could be included.
  2. Ideate how to include visuals created by interviewees in our final project deliverable.
    • We realized that creating a video component would not be feasible with the skills that the group had. Thus, we agreed to ideate and discuss other ways to showcase the drawings created by our interviewees as well as reflect our design knowledge.

The Visuals

Considering many of our group members had experience with working on front-end coding, we settled on a website page to host our visual content, podcast audio, and written reflections through simple HTML and CSS. We sketched out how this site could look like visually.

Podcast Cover Art

We ideated how to approach the podcast cover art, which could be done through Canva. Considering the 15-second self-portraits done in class, we decided to do portraits of each other to create our podcast cover art. Not only would this create an additional visual component to our project, but would allow us as students to reflect on our growth as artists in drawing each other.

Different Pages

However, upon creating the site and hearing about the storytelling aspect that would be required, we decided to create several pages within the website to space out the information and avoid having too much text on one page.

The Final Product

As the quarter picked up in the final weeks we did our best to work on finalizing our project. Kayla created a document outlining the necessary to-dos to accomplish our goal product. Gisele led the podcast-creation process, while Emiri and Roshni worked on the repository to create a visual place to showcase our project.

The Podcast

The podcast-creation process was led by Gisele, who showcased how the podcast would be approached to allow other group members to know what kind of interview audio would be necessary. During this time, Kayla listened to our conversation to identify who's audio we needed for which questions.

We each went through our interview audios to find timestamps of interviewees answering our questions and providing insight. Since there we did not use a collaborative platform to work on the podcast, this would be done all by Gisele. However, by providing timestamps and important quotes to include, the other group members could ease the workload.

Since we envisioned our podcast being an upbeat and happy tone, we wanted to add background music to encourage this. We looked into free music libraries that provided upbeat music to include in our podcast. Furthermore, we recorded a short intro to the podcast to make it more cohesive and professional.

The Repository

After creating a git repository for our final project deliverable, Emiri began coding out the visual look of the site using previous knowledge from front-end development classes. With the help of Kayla's design aesthetic to create a color palette, the design and theme of the site were developed. The different components of our project and descriptions of our project were added to reflect our thought process and lessons learned through the process.

Considering the podcast took the longest amount of time to complete, other portions of the project (visuals, descriptions, reflections, and stories) were added to the site prior to the podcast. This was mainly done by Emiri, Kayla, and Roshni as Gisele focused on creating and finalizing our podcast. Our project was completed after reviewing and inserting the podcast audio.
